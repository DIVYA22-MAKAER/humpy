About Hampi

The forgotten empire

A Historical Gem

Hampi was once the capital of the powerful Vijayanagara Empire. Located in Karnataka, India, this ancient city is now a captivating complex of ruins spread across a stunning landscape of giant boulders, lush greenery, and the Tungabhadra River.

The city reached its peak during the 16th century when it was one of the largest and richest cities in the world. Today, its ruins offer a glimpse into the splendor of its past.

UNESCO Recognition

Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, Hampi is celebrated for its remarkable architecture, religious monuments, civil structures, ancient market streets, and royal complexes.

The site combines historical significance with natural beauty, creating a magical atmosphere that transports visitors back in time. Hampi's monuments represent a unique artistic style that blends elements from various traditions.

Hampi's monuments bear testimony to the wealth, prosperity, and artistic excellence of the Vijayanagara Empire, making it one of India's most fascinating historical destinations.

Distinctive Architectural Features

Hampi's architecture is characterized by its unique blend of styles and incredible craftsmanship.

The temples of Hampi follow the classic Dravidian style, characterized by stepped pyramidal towers, elaborate pillared halls, and intricate carvings. The structures feature ornate pillars, each uniquely carved with images of gods, goddesses, and mythological scenes.

The stone carvings of Hampi are renowned for their detail and expressiveness. From delicate floral motifs to dynamic scenes from Hindu epics, these carvings showcase the exceptional skill of the artisans who worked here centuries ago.

Hampi is famous for its monolithic sculptures, such as the colossal Narasimha statue and the massive Lakshmi Narasimha statue. These impressive structures were carved from single giant boulders, showcasing extraordinary engineering skills.

History Timeline

The story of an empire

1336 CE

Foundation of Empire

The Vijayanagara Empire was founded by Harihara I and Bukka Raya I, who established Hampi as their capital.

1446-1465 CE

Expansion Under Deva Raya II

The empire reached its peak under Deva Raya II, expanding its territory and influence throughout South India.

1509-1529 CE

Golden Age

Under Krishna Deva Raya, Vijayanagara experienced its golden age with flourishing art, architecture, and commerce.

1565 CE

Battle of Talikota

The empire faced defeat at the Battle of Talikota, leading to the sacking of Hampi by allied sultanates.

1800s

Rediscovery

British officials and archaeologists began documenting the ruins of Hampi, bringing them to wider attention.

1986

UNESCO Recognition

Hampi was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural and historical significance.

How to Reach

Planning your journey

By Air

The nearest airports to Hampi are:

  • Ballari Airport (Bellary) - About 60 km from Hampi
  • Hubli Airport - About 160 km from Hampi
  • Bangalore International Airport - About 350 km from Hampi

From these airports, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Hampi.

By Train

The nearest railway stations are:

  • Hospet Junction - About 13 km from Hampi, with good connections to major cities
  • Bellary - About 60 km from Hampi

From Hospet, you can take an auto-rickshaw, taxi, or local bus to Hampi.

By Road

Hampi is well-connected by road to major cities in Karnataka and neighboring states:

  • From Bangalore - 350 km (approx. 8 hours)
  • From Hyderabad - 380 km (approx. 8-9 hours)
  • From Goa - 330 km (approx. 7-8 hours)

Regular buses operate from these cities to Hospet, from where you can reach Hampi.

Local Transportation

Within Hampi, you can get around by:

  • Auto-rickshaws - Available for short distances
  • Bicycles - Can be rented for exploring at your own pace
  • Walking - Many attractions are within walking distance
  • Coracle boats - To cross the Tungabhadra River

Virupaksha Temple

Ancient Living Temple

The Virupaksha Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, known here as Virupaksha, the consort of the local goddess Pampa. It is one of the oldest functioning temples in India, with a history dating back to the 7th century.

The temple complex features:

  • A stunning 50-meter high main gopuram (tower)
  • Intricate stone carvings depicting mythology
  • A sanctum with daily rituals and ceremonies
  • A unique pinhole camera effect in one of its chambers

The temple remains the most important spiritual center in Hampi and continues to attract pilgrims and tourists alike.

Vijaya Vittala Temple

Architectural Marvel

The Vittala Temple is dedicated to Lord Vittala, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Built in the 15th century, it represents the pinnacle of Vijayanagara temple architecture.

Key features include:

  • The famous Stone Chariot, an iconic symbol of Hampi
  • Musical pillars that produce different musical notes when tapped
  • Intricately carved halls with mythological sculptures
  • Beautiful mandapas (pavilions) with detailed reliefs

The temple demonstrates the extraordinary artistic vision and technical skill of the craftsmen of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Lotus Mahal

Elegant Palace Structure

The Lotus Mahal is a unique structure that beautifully blends Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. Located in the Zenana Enclosure, it was part of the royal quarters for women.

Distinctive features include:

  • Lotus-like structure with arched windows and domed roof
  • An advanced cooling system with pipes for water circulation
  • Symmetrical design with delicate carvings
  • One of the few structures in Hampi that wasn't severely damaged

This elegant building was likely used as a relaxation area for royal ladies and showcases the cultural synthesis that characterized the Vijayanagara Empire.

Matanga Hill

Panoramic Viewpoint

Matanga Hill is the highest point in Hampi, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the entire landscape, including temples, ruins, and the Tungabhadra River.

What makes it special:

  • The perfect spot for sunrise and sunset photography
  • A relatively short climb with stone steps leading to the top
  • 360-degree views of Hampi's archaeological complex
  • Home to the ancient Veerabhadra Temple

According to legend, this hill is where the sage Matanga lived and where Sugriva, from the Ramayana epic, took shelter when fleeing from his brother Vali.
